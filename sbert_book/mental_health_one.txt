Mental disorders are diseases that affect cognition, emotion,
and behavioral control and substantially interfere both with the
ability of children to learn and with the ability of adults to
function in their families, at work, and in the broader society.
Mental disorders tend to begin early in life and often run a
chronic recurrent course. They are common in all countries
where their prevalence has been examined. Because of the
combination of high prevalence, early onset, persistence, and
impairment, mental disorders make a major contribution to
